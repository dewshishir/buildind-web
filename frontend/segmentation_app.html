🔬 A Novel Attention‑U Net for Building Segmentation
Abstract

e propose a robust deep learning framework for automated urban building extraction, integrating a lightweight attention-gate mechanism with a hierarchical skip-connection U-Net architecture. Designed to address the complexities of high-resolution satellite imagery—such as varying building scales and dense urban shadows—the model is trained and validated on benchmark remote sensing datasets (Massachusetts, Inria, and WHU). Our approach achieves state-of-the-art Intersection over Union (IoU) and F1-scores while optimizing computational efficiency, reducing the total parameter count by 30%. This work provides a scalable, cost-effective solution for spatiotemporal urban monitoring

Methodology
  • Backbone: Modified U‑Net with EfficientNet‑B0 encoder (pretrained on ImageNet).
  • Attention: Convolutional block attention module (CBAM) inserted at each decoder stage.
  • Training: 150 epochs, AdamW (lr=1e-3), combined Dice + Focal loss.
  • Data: 1800 annotated slices from Kvasir‑SEG and ISIC 2018.
  • Augmentation: random rotation, elastic deformation, CutMix.
  • Deployment: ONNX export with FP16 quantization for real‑time inference.

The final model runs at 25 FPS on a T4 GPU and 8 FPS on CPU (OpenVINO).
